A New Beginning, A Familiar Memory

A quiet story about a new role, a mint pot, and the memory of a friend who never really left.

Some beginnings feel new on the surface, yet deeply connected underneath.

Some moments arrive quietly and stay with you. A small mint pot in a welcome pack did exactly that.

A Gift That Meant More Than Expected

I am starting a new role next week. A new team, a new environment, and a new chapter that fits how I work. Among the welcome materials was a small pot for growing mint. A simple gesture, but one that carried a weight I did not expect.

Mint has always been linked to someone I lost many years ago. His name was Will. He was sharp, thoughtful, and quietly funny in that way that stays with you. He loved plants, understood them deeply, and always dreamed of working at Kew Gardens.

After he passed, I found a bench at Kew dedicated to him. Every year on his anniversary, I go there, sit for a moment, and take in the stillness. It has become a ritual that grounds me. It is never dramatic. Just a pause, a breath, and a moment to acknowledge someone who mattered.

Seeing mint seeds in my welcome pack felt more than coincidental. It felt like a small connection across time, a reminder that memory often waits quietly until it wants to be noticed.

Will's MINT Playlist

There is also a playlist called MINT. It was never debated or curated by one person. Instead, everyone who loved him contributed songs they knew he adored. Tracks that were played during the gathering after the ceremony, and music that reflected his personality, his humour, and his energy.

It is not just music. It is a record of who he was and how he lived. A shared memory, shaped by many people, held together through music.

Memory is not held in a single place. It sits in a bench at Kew, a shared playlist, a song in a tribute video, and even a small mint pot arriving at the right moment.

A Tribute in Music

Years ago, a band called Candy Apple Blue invited listeners to submit photos of loved ones they had lost, for a tribute video to their song Not A Day Goes By. I sent in a picture of Will. Seeing him included in that video felt like a quiet but meaningful tribute, a moment shared far beyond our immediate circle, carried by music. It felt understated, but deeply right.

A Moment That Connected Past and Present

When I opened my welcome pack and saw the mint pot, it brought all of those moments together: the bench at Kew, the playlist, the tribute video, and the people who helped shape those memories.

It reminded me that beginnings do not have to replace what came before. Sometimes they sit alongside the past, acknowledging it without overshadowing it.

Carrying Memory Forward

Taking on this new role feels like the right move: more technical, more structured, and more aligned with what I do best. And yet, this small coincidence made it feel personal in a way I did not anticipate.

We carry our people with us, in habits, in music, in places, and in subtle reminders that appear when we least expect them. Mint, in its simplicity, has tied together part of my past with where I am heading next.

Some stories continue quietly. This one has simply found its next page.
